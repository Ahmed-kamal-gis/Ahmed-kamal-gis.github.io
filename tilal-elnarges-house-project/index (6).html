Project Overview

The Challenge

Water meter locations across a residential area needed to be formally documented and submitted to the National Water Company as a GIS asset file. Field-collected data had to be converted into a spatially accurate, attribute-complete geodatabase that matched NWC's exact schema requirements before it could be accepted for submission.

My Role

Handled the complete process from raw field data to final submission-ready GIS database. This included spatial data entry, attribute structuring, quality control against NWC standards, and final validation before handover to the client.

Key Contributions

Data & Database Work

  • Mapped and documented all water meter locations into a structured GIS layer
  • Organized asset attributes within a standardized geodatabase
  • Formatted all data fields to comply with NWC GIS schema requirements
  • Prepared the final dataset for official submission to the National Water Company

Quality Assurance

  • Validated both spatial accuracy and attribute data completeness
  • Checked all records against NWC acceptance criteria
  • Corrected any inconsistencies identified during QC review
  • Ensured the dataset was ready for long-term asset management use
