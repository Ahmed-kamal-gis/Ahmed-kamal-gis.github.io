Project Overview

The Challenge

Decades-old asbestos water pipelines needed full replacement across multiple districts. All newly installed assets — mains, fire hydrants, valves, and service connections — had to be accurately captured, structured, and submitted to the National Water Company in a clean, error-free GIS format ready for their asset management systems.

My Role

Acted as the GIS specialist responsible for converting raw survey and engineering data into submission-ready infrastructure datasets, while producing AutoCAD As-Built drawings directly from field survey outputs.

Key Contributions

GIS & Data Work

  • Prepared and structured GIS datasets for water mains, fire hydrants, valves, and service connections
  • Validated full network connectivity and eliminated topology errors, overlaps, and gaps
  • Standardized all asset attributes to comply with NWC GIS schema requirements
  • Ensured datasets were submission-ready for integration into the National Water Company's asset management system

CAD & Documentation

  • Produced As-Built drawings directly from field survey data using AutoCAD
  • Documented all new utility assets across multiple replacement zones in Al Thuqbah and Al Khobar
  • Coordinated between survey outputs and GIS layers to maintain spatial accuracy
  • Supported the digital handover of infrastructure assets to the client
