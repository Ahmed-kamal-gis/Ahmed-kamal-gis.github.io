Project Overview

The Challenge

Borehole investigation data collected during geotechnical site studies was spread across separate field records with no unified structure. The National Water Company needed all records consolidated into a GIS database that could be spatially queried, quality-checked, and integrated into future engineering planning.

My Role

Designed and built the GIS spatial database for all borehole records, structured attribute data to meet NWC requirements, and performed systematic quality control checks to ensure accuracy and completeness before submission.

Key Contributions

Database & Spatial Work

  • Created GIS layers to map all borehole locations and associated investigation data
  • Organized and standardized geotechnical attribute information into a clean, queryable schema
  • Structured the geodatabase to comply with National Water Company data requirements
  • Improved long-term traceability of subsurface investigation records

Quality Control

  • Performed detailed QC checks across all spatial and attribute data
  • Identified and corrected inconsistencies in field-recorded information
  • Ensured data accuracy and readiness for integration into NWC systems
  • Made records accessible and reusable for future engineering studies
